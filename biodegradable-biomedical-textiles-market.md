<p><p>The biodegradable biomedical textiles market comprises primarily three categories: non-woven, woven, and others. Non-woven textiles, created by bonding fibers through mechanical, thermal, or chemical processes, offer excellent fluid resistance and are ideal for applications like surgical drapes and masks. Woven textiles, made by interlacing yarns, provide strength and durability, making them suitable for implants and sutures. The 'others' category includes textiles like knitted and engineered fabrics, which cater to specialized medical applications, enhancing versatility in biodegradable solutions.</p></p>
