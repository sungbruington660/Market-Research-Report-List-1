<p><ul><li>Inductive Conductivity Sensor</li><li>Electrodeless Conductivity Sensor</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The water conductivity sensor market primarily features two types: inductive conductivity sensors and electrodeless conductivity sensors. Inductive conductivity sensors utilize magnetic fields to measure conductivity without direct contact with the liquid, ensuring minimal contamination and wear. Conversely, electrodeless conductivity sensors employ electromagnetic principles to assess the ionic content of the water, providing accurate readings while preventing buildup on electrodes. Both types serve critical roles in various applications, including water quality monitoring, industrial processes, and environmental assessments.</p></p>
