<p><p>A P-channel MOSFET transistor is a type of field-effect transistor that uses positive charge carriers, or holes, for conduction. It features three terminals: the source, gate, and drain. When a negative voltage is applied to the gate relative to the source, it allows current to flow from the source to the drain, enabling the device to switch on. This transistor type is commonly used in power management applications, as it offers advantages such as low on-resistance and efficient switching capabilities.</p><p>The P-channel MOSFET transistor market is experiencing significant growth, driven by the increasing demand for energy-efficient solutions in consumer electronics, automotive, and industrial applications.
