<p><p>The Shale Inhibitors market includes types like Plaster, Silicate, Lime, and Potassium Salt, each serving distinct roles in drilling fluids to stabilize shale formations. Plaster (gypsum) prevents clay swelling by providing calcium ions. Silicate forms a protective layer on shale surfaces, reducing water penetration. Lime controls pH and minimizes shale hydration. Potassium Salt, particularly KCl, inhibits clay swelling by replacing sodium ions. These inhibitors enhance drilling efficiency, prevent wellbore instability, and maintain fluid integrity in oil and gas operations.</p></p>
