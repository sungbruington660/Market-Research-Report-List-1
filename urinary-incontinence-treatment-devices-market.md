<p><p>The urinary incontinence treatment devices market includes several key types designed to manage and alleviate symptoms. Artificial urinary sphincters provide mechanical control over urination through inflatable cuffs. Electrical stimulation devices use mild electrical currents to stimulate nerves controlling bladder function. Urethral slings support the urethra to prevent involuntary leakage, while catheters facilitate urine drainage for patients unable to void naturally. Collectively, these devices offer varied options catering to different patient needs and severity of urinary incontinence.</p></p>
