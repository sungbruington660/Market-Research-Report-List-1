<p><p>Hyperspectral remote sensing encompasses various market segments based on spectral ranges. VNIR (Visible Near-Infrared) sensors capture data from the visible spectrum to near-infrared, ideal for vegetation and land-use analysis. SWIR (Short-Wave Infrared) sensors focus on moisture content and minerals, useful in geological and agricultural applications. Thermal LWIR (Long-Wave Infrared) sensors detect heat emissions, enabling temperature monitoring and energy audits. Each segment serves distinct applications, driving advancements in environmental monitoring, agriculture, and urban planning.</p></p>
