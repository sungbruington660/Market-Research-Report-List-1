<p><p>Transdermal and transmucosal drug delivery markets focus on innovative methods for administering medications through the skin or mucosal membranes. Transdermal drug delivery utilizes patches or gels to release drugs into systemic circulation via the skin, providing sustained therapeutic effects and improved patient compliance. In contrast, transmucosal drug delivery involves administering drugs through mucosal surfaces, such as the oral cavity or nasal passages, facilitating rapid absorption and onset of action. Both methods enhance bioavailability and reduce gastrointestinal side effects.</p></p>
